## Passage Translation

Indeed, the world of animals is like the world of humans; it has its own characteristics, nature, and feelings similar to humans in many situations.

Allah the Most High says:

> **"There is no creature on the earth nor bird that flies with its wings except that they are communities like you."**

Just as animals have feelings and needs, showing mercy toward them can become a means of entering Paradise.

The Prophet ﷺ told the story of a man who became extremely thirsty while traveling. He found a well, climbed down into it, drank water, and then came out. Afterward he saw a dog panting heavily and eating dirt because of severe thirst.

The man said:

> "This dog has reached the same level of thirst that I had reached."

He then returned to the well, filled his shoe with water, carried it in his mouth, climbed out, and gave water to the dog.

Because of this act of mercy:

> **Allah appreciated his deed and forgave him.**

The Companions asked:

> "O Messenger of Allah, is there reward for us in helping animals?"

The Prophet ﷺ replied:

> **"Yes, there is reward in every living being."**

On the other hand, cruelty toward animals can become a cause for punishment.

The Prophet ﷺ said:

> **"A woman entered the Fire because of a cat. She neither fed it nor allowed it to eat from the creatures of the earth."**

---

## Key Lessons from Paragraph 1

1. Animals are communities created by Allah just like humans.
2. Animals have feelings, needs, and emotions.
3. Showing mercy to animals can be a cause of entering Paradise.
4. Helping thirsty or hungry animals is rewarded by Allah.
5. Every act of kindness toward a living creature earns reward.
6. Cruelty toward animals can become a cause for punishment.
7. Islam teaches compassion toward all living beings.

## Passage Translation

Allah, the Most High, has subjected many animals for the service of human beings. He says:

> “And the cattle—He created them for you. In them there is warmth and many benefits, and from them you eat. And for you in them is beauty when you bring them home in the evening and when you take them out to pasture. And they carry your loads to lands you could not reach except with great hardship. Indeed, your Lord is Most Kind, Most Merciful. And He created horses, mules, and donkeys for you to ride and as adornment, and He creates what you do not know.”

Islam commands kindness toward animals and forbids mistreating them. Therefore, a person is prohibited from remaining seated on an animal for a long time while it is standing.

The Prophet ﷺ said:

> “Do not use the backs of your animals as pulpits.”

He also forbade overburdening animals beyond their capacity. The Sharia has established rights for animals, and animals must not be driven harshly, beaten severely, or left standing with heavy loads on their backs.

## Passage Translation

Islamic law forbids overworking animals beyond their ability.

Abdullah ibn Ja‘far reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ entered a garden and saw a camel. When the camel saw the Prophet ﷺ, it cried and tears flowed from its eyes.

The Prophet ﷺ asked:

> “Who owns this camel?”

A young man from the Ansar replied:

> “It is mine, O Messenger of Allah.”

The Prophet ﷺ said:

> “Do you not fear Allah regarding this animal? It has complained to me that you keep it hungry and overwork it.”

The Prophet ﷺ also forbade killing birds and animals without a valid reason. He forbade using living creatures as targets for practice and cursed those who did so.

Likewise, Islam forbids branding animals on their faces. When the Prophet ﷺ saw a donkey branded on its face, he said:

> “May Allah curse the one who branded it.”

## Passage Translation

For animals that are lawful to eat, kindness includes sharpening the knife, giving them water, and treating them gently before slaughter.

The Prophet ﷺ said:

> “Indeed, Allah has prescribed excellence in all things. When you kill, do so properly, and when you slaughter, do so properly. Let one of you sharpen his blade and ease his animal.”

The Prophet ﷺ once saw a man laying down a sheep while sharpening his knife and said:

> “Do you want to make it die multiple deaths? Why did you not sharpen your knife before laying it down?”

During a journey, some companions took two chicks from a bird. The mother bird became distressed and fluttered around. The Prophet ﷺ said:

> “Who has distressed this bird by taking its children? Return its children to it.”

The Prophet ﷺ also saw an ant colony that had been burned and said:

> “It is not appropriate that anyone punish with fire except the Lord of the Fire.”

## Passage Translation

Animals were given an important place in Islamic society. Historical charitable endowments were established specifically to care for sick and disabled animals.

One of the finest examples of kindness to animals is the statement of Abu al-Darda’, who said to his camel at the time of his death:

> “O camel, do not complain about me to your Lord, for I never burdened you beyond your capacity.”

Likewise, the Companion ‘Adi ibn Hatim used to feed bread to ants and would say:

> “They are our neighbors, and they have rights over us.”

Similarly, the great scholar Abu Ishaq al-Shirazi was walking with his students when a dog passed by. One of his companions attempted to drive it away, but the Shaykh stopped him and said:

> “Do you not know that the road is shared between us and it?”

# Key Lessons from Lesson 11

2. Animals have rights in Islam.
3. Animals must not be overworked.
4. Animals must not be beaten or mistreated.

6. Animals should not be branded on their faces.
7. Kindness must be shown during slaughter.
8. Children should not be separated from their animal parents.
9. Even ants and birds deserve compassion.
10. Islamic civilization established institutions to care for sick and disabled animals.
